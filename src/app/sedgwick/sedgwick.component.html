LOGLINE

A malevalent ghoul walks the streets of Chicago, embedding itself in the city inhabitants' lives.

STORY

THEMES

"Sedgwick" is about complacency. Anyone who has lived in a city knows there's a lot of suffering we wave away as "just part of city life" — violence, poverty, drug overdoses, lead pipes. They're so routine we often don't think of them as problems to solve anymore. At best, we ignore them; at worst, we find them entertaining.

If this is how an American city handles societal ills...how would they handle the emergence of a monster?

CHARACTERS

Lester

"Sedgwick" is loudly about Lester. While he appears human, he is a ghoul — a haunting, physical entity. Lester ambles aimlessly around the streets of Chicago, completely out in the open. Bold residents take photos of him or follow him until they get bored...or until he attacks.

Q. Why don't police kill him?
A. Because like gun violence or traffic deaths, Lester can't be stopped.
Q. Why doesn't the city evacuate?
A. Lester isn't like Godzilla, wrecking havoc and crushing buildings. He kills 8-10 people per year. So...would you move from your city if the murder rate increased by 8-10 people?

Chicago

"Sedgwick" is quietly about the inhabitants of Chicago, and how we react to society's ills.

  • Abby, a recent transplant to the city, finds Lester grimly fascinating — a topic inappropriate for polite conversation.
  • Devon, a Chicago native, resents the way Lester's reputation has become synonymous with the city's.
  • Raj, an upper-class resident of several years, caustically finds the ghoul an entertaining Chicago anecdote.
  • Vic, originally from the suburbs, is openly disgusted, and takes every opportunity to criticize the city for “allowing” the ghoul’s rampage to occur.
