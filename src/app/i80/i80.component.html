LOGLINE

A bumbling brother and sister seek petty revenge against a freight shipping magnate, drawing the attention of a traveling gun-for-hire seeking to escape her world of violence. When she seizes an opportunity to do so, a series of misunderstandings bring mayhem to a quiet Iowa city.

INTRODUCTION

Interstate 80 is the shag carpeting of freeways. For 2900 ugly, unremarkable miles it connects San Francisco and Teaneck, New Jersey, avoiding every notable geological landmark in between. It doesn’t inspire or amuse. It simply represents the path of least resistance for East-West travel. The only marvels along its route are perhaps the Great Salt Lake, the Great Divide Basin, and a handful of mid-sized cities.

Davenport, Iowa, is not one of those marvels. Like many would-be cities along the interstate, Davenport lives by the predictable rhythm of the freeway. The constant thrum of passing big rigs is interrupted only by the Great Summer Migration of motorists on their way to vacation destinations.

It’s here, where nothing happens on purpose, that everything in this story happens by accident.

It begins with a Gatorade bottle full of piss.

THEMES

Sedgwick is about complacency. Anyone who has lived in a city knows there's a lot of suffering we wave away as "just part of life" — violence, drug overdoses, homelessness, lead pipes. They're so routine that we don't even fear them anymore.

  • To most, homelessness is an annoying blight
  • To some, violence is a point of pride; "I'm from Chi-raq" means "I'm tougher than you"
  • To right-wing commentators, a city struggling to curtail drug addiction is a cudgel to wave at liberal policies

If this is how an American city handles societal ills...how would they handle the emergence of a monster?

Episodes 1-3

In the first act, we are introduced to the two groups of characters: the squabbling Locals of Davenport, Iowa, and the malicious Outsiders who visit terror along the sleepy interstate. After a trucker throws a Gatorade bottle full of piss at Local Jesse Goetsch, he and sibling Mickey lash out against Local shipping magnates Alton and Dawn Eberle. Their petty revenge draws the attention of Outsider hitwoman Mal Ziaja, traveling through town on her way to kill a businessman in Reno.

Meanwhile, Outsider enforcers Vas Reddy and Love Jacunski arrive in Davenport, contracted to collect a debt owed by the Eberles to their employer.

Episodes 4-6

In the second act, the squabbles between the Goetschs and Eberles escalate when Jesse’s friend, Local Ed Mercado, begins a romance with their daughter, Local Rianne Eberle.

Mal, fed up with her life of violence, imprisons Claudia in the back of her trailer and claims her identity. On her way back to Davenport, she severs the last connection to her previous life by killing Gid Depue, her handler.

Vas and Love investigate Mal’s actions and incorrectly conclude the Eberles have hired her to tie up loose ends. Fearing for their lives, they kidnap Rianne to guarantee their safety.

Episodes 7-8

The misunderstandings of the characters reach a fever pitch. Mal’s new life of tranquility is threatened when Claudia escapes. Without a clear enemy, the Eberles hunt the Goetschs, even as the Goetschs themselves have fallen victim to Mal. Vas and Love, thinking their lives are also in danger, bizarrely find themselves with no pursuers at all, leading to a final confrontation wherein no one knows the true motivations of anyone else.
